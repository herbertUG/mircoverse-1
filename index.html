Nelson Mandela

Nelson Mandela dedicated his life to the struggle for justice and equality, and his legacy will forever be remembered as a beacon of hope and inspiration for future generations.

Here's a time line of Dr. Borlaug's life

  1. 1918 - Mandela is born on July 18 in the village of Mvezo, South Africa.

  2. 1939 - Mandela begins studying law at the University of Fort Hare.

  3. 1942 - Mandela is expelled from the University of Fort Hare for participating in a student strike.

  4. 1943 - Mandela begins working as a law clerk in Johannesburg and becomes involved in the African National Congress (ANC), a political organization that promotes the rights of black South Africans.

  5. 1944 - Mandela co-founds the Youth League of the ANC.

  6. 1948 - The National Party, a white supremacist party, comes to power in South Africa and begins implementing the system of racial segregation known as apartheid.

  8. 1944 - Mandela co-founds the Youth League of the ANC.

  10. 1952 - Mandela is arrested and charged with inciting workers to strike. He is found guilty and sentenced to nine months' imprisonment, suspended for two years.

  11. 1956 - Mandela is arrested and charged with high treason for his involvement in the ANC. He is acquitted in 1961.

  12. 1962 - Mandela is arrested again and sentenced to five years in prison for leaving the country illegally and inciting workers to strike.

  13. 1964 - Mandela is put on trial again and sentenced to life in prison for sabotage and conspiracy to overthrow the government. He is sent to the maximum security prison on Robben Island.

  14. 1990 - Mandela is released from prison after serving 27 years.

  15. 1991 - Mandela becomes the president of the ANC.

  16. 1994 - Mandela is elected the first black President of South Africa in the country's first fully democratic elections.

  17. 1998 - Mandela steps down as President of South Africa.

  18. 1999 - Mandela retires from public life.

  19. 2013 - Mandela dies on December 5 at the age of 95.
